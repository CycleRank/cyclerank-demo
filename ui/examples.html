Reasons why we felt this demo was needed.

The Problem


Surfing the links between Wikipedia articles constitutes a valuable way to acquire new knowledge related to a topic by exploring its connections to other pages. Personalized PageRank is a well-known option to make sense of the graph of links between pages, and identify the most relevant articles with respect to a given one; its performance, however, is hindered by pages with high indegree that function as hubs and obtain high scores regardless of the starting point.

See the results in detail. One can easily notice how prominent the node "World War II" is, even though the source node for the Personalized PageRank was "Fake news".

Our Solution


The solution is found in CycleRank, a novel algorithm based on cyclic paths aimed at finding the most relevant nodes related to a topic.

See the results in details. CycleRank easily overcomes the above-mentioned issue. The same query launched with this new algorithm, source node "Fake news" and max cycle length 3 leads to results that aren't polluted by high in-degree articles.

For the Curious


This demo is capable of showing the results of the PageRank, Single Source Personalized PageRank and Cyclerank algorithms, but doesn't stop there. Two other algorithms are implemented: CheiRank and 2DRank, both in their personalized and normal forms. CheiRank, depending on which version is chosen, runs the PageRank or the Single Source PageRank algorithm on the transposed version of the graph. 2DRank combines the results of the PageRank algorithm with the ones of the CheiRank algorithm, thus creating better rankings at the cost of longer computation time.

See the results in details. This set of queries show how these other algorithms perform on the same dataset and node as the queries shown before.
